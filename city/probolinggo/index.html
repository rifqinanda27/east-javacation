Probolinggo : City of Mangrove

Probolinggo : City in East Java

Probolinggo City, nestled in East Java, Indonesia, is renowned for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and vibrant atmosphere. Serving as a gateway to iconic destinations like Mount Bromo, the city offers a bustling urban center complemented by scenic landscapes and historical landmarks. From the lively Probolinggo Square to the serene mangrove forests that earned it the moniker "City of Mangrove," Probolinggo presents a diverse array of experiences for visitors. Its rich cultural heritage is reflected in its architecture, traditions, and culinary delights, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in the warmth of Javanese hospitality. Whether exploring bustling markets, enjoying outdoor adventures, or savoring local delicacies, Probolinggo City promises an unforgettable journey through the heart of East Java's charm and allure.

History of Probolinggo

Probolinggo, situated in East Java, Indonesia, has a history steeped in maritime trade and cultural exchange. Initially established as a fishing village, Probolinggo's strategic location along the northern coast of Java made it a thriving port for trading ships navigating the waters of the Java Sea. Over time, the city grew into a bustling commercial center, attracting merchants and traders from across the archipelago and beyond. Its prosperity was further enhanced by the arrival of Chinese, Arab, and Indian settlers, who brought with them diverse cultural influences that enriched Probolinggo's social fabric and contributed to its cosmopolitan character.

During the colonial period, Probolinggo came under Dutch control as part of the Dutch East Indies, experiencing rapid urban development and modernization. The city's port facilities were expanded, and infrastructure projects were initiated to facilitate trade and transportation. Despite the challenges posed by World War II and Indonesia's struggle for independence, Probolinggo emerged as a key economic hub in East Java, with its maritime industry continuing to play a pivotal role in the region's economy. Today, Probolinggo stands as a testament to its storied past, with remnants of its maritime heritage and cultural diversity evident in its architecture, traditions, and way of life.

Popular Destination

1. Mount Bromo

Mount Bromo, situated in East Java, Indonesia, is one of the country's most famous natural attractions. This active volcano is part of the Tengger Massif and stands at an altitude of 2,329 meters (7,641 feet) above sea level. What makes Mount Bromo so captivating is its otherworldly landscape, characterized by a vast caldera surrounded by rugged terrain and towering cliffs. Visitors flock to Mount Bromo to witness the spectacular sunrise views over the caldera, where the morning light casts a golden glow over the volcanic landscape, creating a mesmerizing sight that is truly unforgettable.

Aside from its scenic beauty, Mount Bromo offers opportunities for outdoor adventures, including hiking, horseback riding, and 4x4 jeep tours. Many visitors opt to hike to the crater rim to peer into the smoking crater and take in panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Additionally, the area surrounding Mount Bromo is home to the Tenggerese people, an indigenous community known for their rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending traditional ceremonies and exploring the villages scattered throughout the region. With its stunning vistas and cultural significance, Mount Bromo is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking to experience the beauty and diversity of Indonesia's natural landscape.

2. Madakaripura Waterfall

Hidden within the verdant forests of East Java, Madakaripura Waterfall is a mesmerizing cascade that plunges from a towering cliff into a serene pool below. The journey to reach this natural wonder is an adventure in itself, as visitors traverse narrow paths, wade through shallow streams, and navigate rocky terrain amidst the lush vegetation of the surrounding canyon.


Upon arriving at the base of the waterfall, travelers are greeted by the thunderous roar of cascading water and enveloped in a refreshing mist that fills the air. With its ethereal beauty and tranquil ambiance, Madakaripura offers a peaceful retreat where visitors can reconnect with nature and marvel at the sheer power and magnificence of Indonesia's natural landscapes.

3. Probolinggo Square

As the vibrant heart of the city, Probolinggo Square pulsates with energy and activity, attracting locals and visitors alike to its bustling streets and lively atmosphere. Here, travelers can immerse themselves in the vibrant culture of Probolinggo, exploring colorful market stalls selling a variety of goods, from fresh produce to traditional handicrafts and souvenirs.


Street food vendors offer an array of delicious local delicacies, tempting passersby with their aromatic offerings and savory snacks. Additionally, the square serves as a venue for community events, festivals, and cultural performances, providing entertainment and opportunities for socializing and relaxation amidst the dynamic urban landscape of Probolinggo.

4. Sumber Maron Waterfall

Nestled amidst the serene countryside surrounding Probolinggo, Sumber Maron Waterfall offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The journey to reach the waterfall takes visitors through picturesque landscapes of rolling hills, lush forests, and quaint villages, providing a scenic backdrop for outdoor exploration and adventure.


Upon arriving at the waterfall, travelers are rewarded with a breathtaking sight as the crystal-clear waters cascade gracefully down the rocky cliffs into a pristine pool below. Visitors can cool off with a refreshing swim in the cool waters, relax amidst the tranquil surroundings, or simply marvel at the natural beauty of the cascading falls. With its secluded location and untouched wilderness, Sumber Maron Waterfall offers a serene sanctuary where visitors can unwind and reconnect with the beauty of nature in Probolinggo.
