Malang : City in East Java

Malang City, located in East Java, Indonesia, is a bustling urban hub known for its history and natural beauty. The city streets showcase old buildings from Dutch colonial times, and its markets buzz with local culture. Malang is also a big center for education, with many schools and universities attracting students from all over. Around the city, there are stunning landscapes like Mount Bromo and waterfalls to explore. Food is a big deal here too, with tasty local dishes like Bakso Malang to try. With its pleasant weather and lots of fun things to see and do, Malang is a great place to visit in East Java.

History of Malang

Malang City, nestled in the heart of East Java, Indonesia, has a storied past that spans centuries. Initially inhabited by indigenous communities, Malang evolved into a thriving settlement during the Hindu-Buddhist period. However, it wasn't until the Dutch colonial era in the 17th century that Malang truly flourished, becoming a vital trading hub due to its strategic location along major trade routes. The Dutch left a lasting architectural legacy, evident in the colonial-era buildings that still line the streets of Malang's historic district.

After Indonesia gained independence in the 20th century, Malang continued to grow, transforming into a bustling urban center known for its rich cultural heritage and educational institutions. Today, Malang stands as a vibrant fusion of tradition and modernity, offering visitors a glimpse into its fascinating history through its architectural landmarks, bustling markets, and diverse cultural attractions.

Popular Destination

1. Coban Rondo Waterfall

Coban Rondo Waterfall is a stunning natural attraction located in East Java, Indonesia, near Malang. Nestled amidst lush tropical greenery, this waterfall is renowned for its picturesque beauty and tranquil ambiance. The name "Coban Rondo" translates to "Widow's Waterfall" in the local Javanese language, adding a touch of mystique to its allure.

The waterfall cascades down a series of rocky cliffs, creating a mesmerizing display of rushing water and mist. Visitors can enjoy the refreshing spray of the waterfall while immersing themselves in the surrounding natural scenery. The area around Coban Rondo is also adorned with vibrant flora, adding to the enchanting atmosphere.

2. Sempu Island

Sempu Island is a hidden gem located off the southern coast of Java, Indonesia, near the city of Malang. It is a protected nature reserve and is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and lush tropical forests. One of the island's most iconic features is Segara Anakan Lagoon, a saltwater lake located in the center of the island that is separated from the ocean by a narrow strip of land. The lagoon is surrounded by dense jungle and is a popular destination for swimming and snorkeling.


One of the highlights of visiting Sempu Island is the journey itself. Accessible only by boat, visitors must first travel to Sendang Biru Beach on the mainland, where they can rent boats or join guided tours to reach the island. The boat ride offers stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding sea, adding to the sense of adventure.

3. Alun - alun Tugu

Alun-Alun Tugu Malang stands as the lively heart of Malang, East Java, Indonesia, offering a vibrant space for community gatherings and leisure activities. At its center, the iconic Tugu Monument, also known as "Tugu Malang" or "Bambu Runcing," proudly symbolizes the city's rich history and cultural heritage. Surrounded by lush greenery, benches, and pathways, the square provides a welcoming environment for locals and visitors alike to relax, socialize, and enjoy the bustling atmosphere. Throughout the year, Alun-Alun Tugu Malang comes alive with various events, including music concerts, art exhibitions, and traditional festivals, adding to its charm and cultural significance.


Flanked by shops, cafes, and restaurants, Alun-Alun Tugu Malang also serves as a popular destination for shopping and dining experiences. Visitors can explore the diverse offerings of local handicrafts and souvenirs, indulge in a culinary adventure, or simply soak in the sights and sounds of this dynamic urban space. Whether it's immersing oneself in the local culture or simply enjoying a leisurely stroll, Alun-Alun Tugu Malang invites everyone to experience the lively spirit of Malang city in the heart of its bustling square.

4. The Coban Rondo Labyrinth

The Coban Rondo Labyrinth is a unique attraction located near Coban Rondo Waterfall in East Java, Indonesia. This labyrinth, also known as the "Hedge Maze," offers visitors an entertaining and adventurous experience as they navigate through a maze of tall hedges and pathways. Designed to challenge and delight visitors of all ages, the labyrinth provides an opportunity for exploration and discovery amidst the lush surroundings of the waterfall area.


As visitors venture into the labyrinth, they encounter twists, turns, and dead ends, adding an element of excitement and mystery to the journey. The maze offers a fun-filled adventure for families, friends, and nature enthusiasts alike, providing a memorable experience that complements the natural beauty of Coban Rondo Waterfall.
