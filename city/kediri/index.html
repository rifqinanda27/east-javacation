Kediri : City in East Java

Kediri City, nestled in East Java, Indonesia, encapsulates a rich tapestry of history, culture, and modernity. With roots tracing back to ancient Javanese kingdoms like the Majapahit Empire, the city boasts well-preserved landmarks such as the Kediri Palace ruins and the Penataran Temple, which stand as testaments to its illustrious past. Beyond its historical treasures, Kediri pulsates with a vibrant cultural scene, where traditional arts, music, and dance flourish, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in the local heritage through dynamic performances and festivals. Moreover, as a bustling commercial and economic hub, Kediri's bustling markets, shopping centers, and diverse culinary offerings reflect the city's dynamic urban landscape, offering travelers an opportunity to experience the blend of tradition and modernity that defines this captivating destination in East Java.

History of Kediri

Kediri City in East Java, Indonesia, has a rich and storied history that stretches back centuries. It was once a prominent center of power during the era of the ancient Javanese kingdoms, particularly under the rule of the mighty Majapahit Empire. The city flourished as a hub of trade, culture, and governance, leaving behind significant historical remnants such as the ruins of the Kediri Palace (Kota Lama Kediri) and the Penataran Temple (Candi Penataran), which stand as enduring symbols of its past glory. Over time, Kediri's fortunes ebbed and flowed with changes in political power and regional dynamics, but it retained its cultural significance and continued to play a pivotal role in shaping the identity of East Java.

In more recent history, Kediri has undergone urban development and transformation, evolving into a bustling modern city while still preserving its heritage and traditions. Today, Kediri stands as a vibrant urban center with a blend of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and modern amenities, offering visitors a glimpse into its rich tapestry of the past and present.

Popular Destination

1. The Kediri Palace Ruins

The Kediri Palace Ruins, also known as Kota Lama Kediri, stand as a poignant reminder of Kediri's illustrious past as a center of power and culture during the era of the Majapahit Empire. This archaeological site, nestled in the heart of Kediri City, offers visitors a captivating glimpse into the city's rich historical legacy. The ruins comprise the remnants of the ancient palace complex, including stone foundations, walls, and sculptures, which bear witness to the grandeur and sophistication of Kediri's royal court. As visitors explore the site, they can envision the bustling activity and vibrant culture that once thrived within the palace walls, gaining insights into the city's significance as a hub of Javanese civilization.

Today, the Kediri Palace Ruins serve as a cultural and historical landmark, attracting tourists and history enthusiasts alike. Interpretive displays and signage scattered throughout the site provide valuable context and information about the palace's history and architectural features. Visitors can wander through the ancient corridors, imagining the lives of the royal inhabitants who once walked these hallowed grounds. With its atmospheric ambiance and rich historical significance, the Kediri Palace Ruins offer a unique opportunity to delve into the past and unravel the mysteries of Kediri's royal legacy.

2. Penataran Temple

Penataran Temple, nestled amidst the serene slopes of Mount Kelud in Kediri, is a significant cultural landmark that showcases Indonesia's ancient heritage. Built in the 12th century, this Hindu temple complex impresses visitors with its grand architecture and intricate stone carvings depicting mythical stories and religious ceremonies. As you wander through the temple grounds, you'll encounter towering stone structures and sacred shrines dedicated to various deities, offering a glimpse into the spiritual beliefs of Indonesia's past civilizations.


Penataran Temple holds a special place in the hearts of many Indonesians, who come here to pay respects to the gods and seek blessings for prosperity and well-being. With its tranquil ambiance and rich historical significance, the temple serves as a sacred sanctuary where visitors can immerse themselves in the country's ancient traditions and connect with its cultural roots.

3. Bukit Kelud

Bukit Kelud, located near Mount Kelud in Kediri, is a scenic hill offering panoramic views of the surrounding volcanic landscape. It's a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers seeking to immerse themselves in the beauty of East Java's countryside. Visitors can hike to the hill's summit or take leisurely walks along its trails, enjoying the fresh air and lush greenery along the way.


At the top, you'll be rewarded with breathtaking vistas of Mount Kelud and its volcanic terrain, providing an unforgettable backdrop for outdoor exploration and photography. Whether you're seeking a challenging hike or a peaceful retreat amidst nature, Bukit Kelud promises an invigorating experience for visitors of all ages.

4. Watu Ondo Waterfall

Watu Ondo Waterfall, found in the forests of Kediri, is a peaceful spot perfect for nature lovers. A trek through the jungle leads you to this hidden gem, where you'll hear the soothing sound of rushing water.


The waterfall cascades down rugged cliffs, creating a beautiful scene in the midst of lush greenery. Visitors often enjoy swimming in the cool pool beneath the falls or simply relaxing in the tranquil surroundings. It's a serene retreat where you can unwind and connect with nature's beauty, making it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a peaceful escape.
