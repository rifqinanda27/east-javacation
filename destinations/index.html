Destination Highlight

Enjoy beauty of East Java Destination

Mount Bromo

Mount Bromo

Indonesia has a well-known volcano called Bromo. It is a component of the Tengger Massif, a collection of volcanoes. Bromo's breathtaking views are the reason why people adore going there. You can see a large stretch of sand surrounding the volcano at sunrise. Bromo can occasionally even emit smoke and ash, adding to its spectacular appearance. In order to take in the breathtaking view, many visitors climb up to the crater's edge.

Ijen Krater

Ijen Krater

The Ijen Crater is a big hole in Indonesia with a cool blue lake inside. The lake looks special because it's super acidic and full of sulfur, which gives it a bright turquoise color. Early in the morning, you can see blue flames flickering around the crater, like magic! People also come here to see how locals mine sulfur from the volcano, even though it's hard work. The place is really pretty, with green hills all around.

De Djawatan Forest

De Djawatan Forest

De Djawatan Forest, or Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda, is a big forest park in Bandung, Indonesia. People love it for its beautiful trees, animals, and great trails for walking. It's named after a famous Indonesian leader. You can hike, have picnics, and see lots of plants and animals there. It's a peaceful place to relax away from the busy city.

Madakaripura Waterfall

Madakaripura Waterfall

Madakaripura Waterfall in East Java, Indonesia, is the tallest waterfall there. It's like a hidden gem, tucked among green cliffs. People love to visit because it's stunning! Legend has it that a famous warrior once meditated here. To get there, you'll trek through a canyon and splash through streams. It's a real adventure! And the sound of the waterfall is so peaceful. Everyone who loves nature should see it!

Wedi Klopo Beach

Wedi Klopo Beach

Wedi Klopo Beach is a beautiful spot in Yogyakarta, Indonesia. It's famous for its soft, sandy shores and clear blue waters. Visitors can relax on the beach, swim, or even try surfing if they're feeling adventurous. The beach is surrounded by coconut trees, giving it a tropical feel. It's a great place to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of Indonesia!

Mount Kelud

Mount Kelud

Mount Kelud is a volcano located in East Java, Indonesia. It's known for its frequent eruptions throughout history, with the most recent significant eruption occurring in 2014. The volcano has a large crater at its summit, which sometimes contains a lake. Mount Kelud's eruptions often result in the release of volcanic ash and rocks, posing a risk to nearby communities. Despite its dangers, Mount Kelud also attracts tourists who are interested in witnessing its natural beauty and learning about its geological history.

Tumpak Sewu Waterfall

Tumpak Sewu Waterfall

Tumpak Sewu Beach, also known as Tumpak Sewu Waterfall, is a stunning natural attraction located in East Java, Indonesia. It's famous for its majestic waterfall cascading down from lush green cliffs, creating a breathtaking sight.

Papuma Beach

Papuma Beach

Papuma Beach is a stunning destination located in Jember, East Java, Indonesia. It's known for its pristine white sand, clear turquoise waters, and dramatic cliffs that overlook the ocean. Visitors can relax on the beach, swim in the calm waters, or explore the surrounding area.

Bondowoso Carter

Bondowoso Carter

The Bondowoso Crater is a stunning natural attraction located in Bondowoso, East Java, Indonesia. It's known for its vast, barren landscape with rugged terrain and steep cliffs. The crater was formed by a volcanic eruption long ago, leaving behind a deep, bowl-shaped depression. Visitors can hike or drive to the edge of the crater to admire the breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Malang City

Malang City

Malang City is a vibrant city located in East Java, Indonesia. It's known for its cool climate, beautiful landscapes, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore historical sites like the Singosari Temple and Jago Temple, which date back to the ancient kingdoms of Java. The city is also famous for its delicious local cuisine, such as bakso (meatball soup) and rawon (beef soup).

The Coban Rondo Labyrinth

The Coban Rondo Labyrinth

The Coban Rondo Labyrinth is a fun and unique attraction located in Batu, Indonesia. It's essentially a maze made up of green hedges and pathways that twist and turn. Visitors can explore the labyrinth, trying to find their way out while enjoying the lush greenery all around them. It's a great place for families and friends to have an adventure together and see who can navigate the maze the fastest!

Sumber Umbulan

Sumber Umbulan

Sumber Umbulan is a natural spring located in East Java, Indonesia. It's known for its crystal-clear water and serene surroundings. People visit Sumber Umbulan to relax and enjoy the refreshing water, which is believed to have therapeutic properties. The area around the spring is lush with greenery, making it a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. It's a popular spot for picnics and nature walks, where visitors can immerse themselves in the beauty of the Indonesian countryside.

Bulak Village

Bulak Village

Bulak Village is a charming rural area located in Indonesia, known for its picturesque landscapes and traditional way of life. Surrounded by lush green fields and small streams, it offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The village is home to friendly locals who engage in farming and handicrafts, preserving their cultural heritage.

Gong Caves

Gong Caves

The Gong Caves, located in Pacitan, East Java, Indonesia, are fascinating natural formations that attract many visitors. These caves are renowned for their unique limestone structures, stalactites, and stalagmites, which create stunning underground landscapes. Exploring the Gong Caves offers an adventurous experience as visitors can walk through narrow passages and marvel at the intricate formations illuminated by dim lighting.

Watukarang Beach

Watukarang Beach

Watu Karang Beach is a stunning beach located in Pacitan, Indonesia. It's known for its picturesque cliffs and clear turquoise waters. Visitors can relax on the sandy shore, swim in the refreshing ocean, or explore the caves and rock formations along the coastline. The beach is surrounded by lush greenery, adding to its natural charm. It's a perfect destination for beach lovers and nature enthusiasts looking for a peaceful retreat.

Onga'an Hill

Onga'an Hill

The Ongaan Hills are a scenic area located in the countryside of Bangladesh. These hills are known for their lush greenery and gentle slopes, making them perfect for hiking and enjoying nature. Visitors can trek through the hills, taking in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape and breathing in the fresh air. The area is also home to diverse wildlife and colorful flora, adding to its charm. Whether you're a nature enthusiast or just looking for a peaceful retreat, the Ongaan Hills offer a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Selomangleng Caves

Selomangleng Caves

Selomangeng Caves are fascinating underground caverns located in Pacitan, Indonesia. These caves are known for their stunning limestone formations, which create unique shapes and patterns throughout the caverns. Visitors can explore the caves and marvel at the natural beauty inside. Some parts of the caves are illuminated, allowing visitors to see the intricate details of the formations. It's a great destination for adventurers and nature lovers alike, offering a glimpse into the mysterious world beneath the earth's surface.

Dolo Waterfall

Dolo Waterfall

Dolo Waterfall is a stunning natural attraction located in Indonesia. It's known for its picturesque scenery and refreshing waters. The waterfall cascades down from a height, creating a mesmerizing sight and providing a cool mist in the surrounding area. Visitors can hike through lush greenery to reach the waterfall and enjoy swimming or simply basking in the tranquility of nature. It's a perfect spot for relaxation and outdoor adventure!
